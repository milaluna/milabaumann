flow state

A collection of movements

A manifest for the body-mind relationship

Flow state is a space where the body-mind relationship is exposed. It gives you the opportunity to explore and to learn.

While it is a place to learn, Flow State doesn’t provide an answer for how your body-mind relationship should be. After all I can only draw on my own knowledge and experience, which is very personal and highly biased. What Flow State does offer is a place where you can encounter the body-mind relationship.

Initially the installation functions as a discussion piece. It allows us to experience and address something that is usually hidden by habits. This is how it will function in the movie and during the exhibition.

In the long run it can become like a shrine for the body-mind relationship. It can be a space that helps us to encounter it and explore it. Like what improvisation does for me Flow State can become for others. A place we feel save to experiment and that we can go back to whenever we feel curious. And once you feel ready you can take this exploration outside of Flow State and into your daily life.

The manifest

Flow State started with the question, how can I create an environment that reacts to the movements of the body? To find an answer I collected movements and explored how they could be connected together. The flick of a finger or the flex of a foot becomes linked to the rising and falling of ‘wings’. The folding of two pieces of wood becomes a joint effort with the press of a hand.

The resulting collection of movements embodies my research, but it also became a method to create endless choreographies and compositions. Using pulleys and ropes I connect points across space and make them move together. By defining how these different points relate to each other I can design how they will move.
