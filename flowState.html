Flow State

'A collection of movements performed by wooden plates, ropes, hinges, the body and the mind'

“Walking forward I feel wooden plates pressing against my skin. Soft fabric touches my arms as I slide into place. My feet are planted firmly on the ground. I lean forward and feel them slip. I arch my back. The wooden plates lift me up. At the verge of falling I push my upper legs into the wood and allow my waist to rise. My feet no longer carry my weight. Instead I reach a balance in how my legs, waist and the wooden plates support me.“

In Flow state design replaces language. With it I explore the body-mind relationship. I start off the conversation with my installation, where body, mind and object can move as one. The project is both an exploration into what the body-mind relationship means and how we can discuss something that is largely understood through experience. My goal was to take the knowledge I gained through sports, tai chi and music and to make that accessible for the broader audience. Not just for those that train their body and mind, but also for those that struggle to connect the two.

For a more detailed explanation of the project, the full movie and some of the research visit here.

Flow State is featured in the online exhibition 'Hit a Wall Break a Leg' as well as the graduation show of the Kabk from the 11th till 13th of september.
